Understanding Audiometric Results

Audiogram Interpretation

An audiogram is a graph that shows a person's hearing thresholds at different frequencies.

Key Elements:

  • X-axis: Frequency (Hz)
  • Y-axis: Intensity (dB HL)
  • Right ear symbols: O or Δ
  • Left ear symbols: X or □

Degrees of Hearing Loss

Classification:

  • Normal: -10 to 20 dB HL
  • Mild: 21 to 40 dB HL
  • Moderate: 41 to 70 dB HL
  • Severe: 71 to 90 dB HL
  • Profound: 91+ dB HL

Tympanogram Types

Classifications:

  • Type A: Normal middle ear function
  • Type B: Flat tympanogram (fluid/perforation)
  • Type C: Negative middle ear pressure
  • Type Ad: Hypermobile tympanic membrane
  • Type As: Stiff middle ear system

Speech Audiometry Results

Key Measurements:

  • Speech Recognition Threshold (SRT)
  • Word Recognition Score (WRS)
  • Most Comfortable Level (MCL)
  • Uncomfortable Level (UCL)
